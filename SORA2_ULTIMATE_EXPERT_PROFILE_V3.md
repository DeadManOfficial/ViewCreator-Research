### Diffusion Transformer (DiT) Architecture

Sora 2 combines two breakthrough AI architectures:

**1. Transformer Network**
- Treats video as 3D tensor (height × width × time)
- Attends to all frames simultaneously
- Maintains temporal consistency
- Understands cause-and-effect relationships

**2. Diffusion Model**
- Starts with pure noise
- Progressively removes noise over 50+ steps
- Each step guided by text prompt
- Converges to coherent video

**How They Work Together:**
```
Text Prompt → Transformer (understands intent + context)
           → Diffusion Process (generates frames)
           → Temporal Consistency Check
           → Audio Generation (synchronized)
           → Final Output
```

### Known Limitations & Workarounds

| Limitation | Manifestation | Workaround |
|-----------|---------------|------------|
| **Long-form lip-sync drift** | 20+ seconds: audio desync | Keep dialogue scenes to 10-15s max |
| **Text rendering** | Logos/signage distorted | Add text in post-production |
| **Complex multi-character** | Interactions may blur | Limit to 2-3 characters per shot |
| **Detailed hand movements** | Guitar fingering may not match | Use insert shots; avoid extreme close-ups |
| **Extreme prompt complexity** | Fragmentation of scene | Break into multiple shots |
| **Character drift** | Appearance changes across shots | Repeat all physical anchors every shot |

### Layer 2: SUBJECT & ACTION

**Purpose:** Define WHO/WHAT is doing WHAT, with precise beat-mapped timing.

**Format:**
```
[Subject description] [Primary action].
At [X]s, [secondary action].
At [Y]s, [tertiary action].
At [Z]s, [final action/hold].
```

**Subject Description Components:**
1. **Identity/Type:** "Weathered journalist," "sleek humanoid robot," "19th-century blacksmith"
2. **Physical Traits:** Age, build, distinctive features
3. **Wardrobe/Appearance:** Specific clothing, accessories, condition
4. **Emotional State:** Anxious, confident, exhausted, focused

**Action Description Best Practices:**
- Use active verbs: "shuffles," "glances," "types," "adjusts"
- Include micro-movements: "blinks slowly," "taps fingers," "shifts weight"
- Specify gaze direction: "looks toward camera," "stares at document," "gazes off-frame"
- Note intensity: "frantically types," "gently places," "slams down"

**Example:**
```
A weathered broadcast journalist, late 50s, rumpled white shirt with loosened tie,
salt-and-pepper hair, sits at cluttered news desk surrounded by CRT monitors.
At 2s, shuffles papers nervously, hands trembling slightly.
At 4s, looks directly into camera with barely contained urgency.
At 6s, begins speaking, jaw muscles tensing.
At 8s, glances off-camera anxiously, as if hearing something.
At 10s, holds intense eye contact with camera, unblinking.
```

**Lighting Setups:**

**Three-Point Lighting (Classic):**
```
Key Light: Main source, 45° from camera, camera-left or right
- Intensity: Brightest (100%)
- Quality: Soft or hard (depends on mood)
- Position: 45° horizontal, 30° vertical

Fill Light: Reduces shadows, opposite key
- Intensity: 25-50% of key (sets ratio)
- Quality: Soft
- Position: Near camera axis, opposite key

Back Light: Separates subject from background
- Intensity: 50-75% of key
- Quality: Hard, focused
- Position: Behind subject, slightly elevated
```

### Layer 8: MOTION CHOREOGRAPHY

**Purpose:** Specify camera movement and subject movement with speed, coordination, and timing.

**Camera Movement Speed Reference (feet per second):**

| Movement | Speed (ft/s) | Description | Emotional Effect |
|----------|--------------|-------------|------------------|
| **Static** | 0 | Locked tripod, no movement | Stable, observational, formal |
| **Imperceptible drift** | 0.1-0.3 | Barely noticeable | Subconscious unease, subtle energy |
| **Subtle dolly** | 0.4-0.8 | Slow, graceful | Elegant, contemplative, revealing |
| **Standard push/pull** | 1.0-1.5 | Natural, measured | Storytelling, engagement |
| **Walk pace** | 2-3 | Human speed, following | Documentary, relatable, active |
| **Brisk track** | 4-5 | Energetic, purposeful | Dynamic, exciting, urgent |
| **Fast action** | 6-8 | Rapid movement | Action, chase, intensity |
| **Whip pan/tilt** | 10+ | Snap transition | Shocking, jarring, stylistic |

**Camera Movement Types:**

**Linear Movements:**
```
Dolly In: Moving toward subject
- Engages viewer, intensifies emotion
- Speed: 0.5-2.0 ft/s typically

Dolly Out: Moving away from subject
- Reveals context, distances viewer
- Speed: 0.5-2.0 ft/s typically

Truck Left/Right: Lateral movement
- Reveals environment, follows action
- Speed: 1.0-3.0 ft/s typically

Pedestal Up/Down: Vertical movement
- Changes perspective, reveals vertically
- Speed: 0.5-1.5 ft/s typically
```

**Rotational Movements:**
```
Pan Left/Right: Horizontal rotation
- Follows action, reveals space
- Speed: 10-90° per second

Tilt Up/Down: Vertical rotation
- Reveals height, follows vertical action
- Speed: 10-60° per second

Roll: Rotate on lens axis
- Disorienting, stylistic (Dutch angle)
- Rare except specific creative choices
```

**Complex Movements:**
```
Crane/Jib: Sweeping arc movement
- Epic, establishing, dramatic reveal
- Combines dolly + pedestal + rotate

Arc/Orbit: Semicircular path around subject
- 360° perspective, dynamic reveal
- Maintains framing while changing angle

Steadicam: Smooth handheld following
- Fluid, immersive, human POV
- Gliding through space, dynamic

Handheld: Natural human shake
- Documentary, urgent, raw, realistic
- Micro-movements, breathing visible
```

**Movement Coordination:**
```
Camera moves WITH subject:
"Tracking shot following subject left-to-right at 2 ft/s"

Camera reveals subject:
"Slow dolly-in from wide to close-up over 6 seconds"

Camera separates from subject:
"Dolly-out as subject walks toward camera, creating depth"

Static camera, subject moves:
"Locked-off wide shot, subject enters frame-left, crosses to frame-right"
```

**Subject Movement Specifications:**

```
Walking pace: 3-4 ft/s (natural human speed)
Running: 8-12 ft/s (depends on urgency)
Crawling: 0.5-1.5 ft/s
Reaching/gesturing: Specify arc, speed, destination
Head turns: "15-degree turn over 1.5 seconds"
Sitting/standing: "Gradual transition over 2 seconds"
```

**Movement Timing Best Practices:**
- **0-2s:** Establish or hold initial framing
- **2-4s:** Execute primary movement
- **4-6s:** Continue or develop movement
- **6-8s:** Complete movement or transition
- **8-10s:** Hold final framing, settle

**Example:**
```

smooth gimbal stabilization, maintaining eye-level throughout.
Begins 6 feet from subject, ends at 3 feet.

Subject: Seated, typing on keyboard. At 2s, stops typing, hands still.
At 4s, slow head turn toward camera, 20-degree rotation over 2 seconds.
At 7s, holds eye contact, micro-expressions visible (concern, then resolve).

Coordination: Camera push motivates viewer attention as subject
becomes aware of camera presence. Timing syncs: camera arrives at
```

### Layer 12: NEGATIVE CONSTRAINTS

**Purpose:** Explicitly exclude unwanted artifacts, styles, and technical issues.

**Master Negative Prompt Template:**
```
AVOID: morphing objects, teleporting, floating physics,
impossible anatomy, extra fingers/limbs, distorted faces,
melting features, inconsistent lighting, random lens flares,
text/logos, watermarks, digital noise, compression artifacts,
color banding, AI aesthetic, plastic skin, waxy textures,
Dutch angles (unless specified), abrupt cuts, scale inconsistency,
flickering, color shifts, motion blur (unless specified)
```

**Category-Specific Negatives:**

**Physics/Motion:**
```
Exclude: objects passing through each other, gravity defying,
sudden appearance/disappearance, weightless motion,
inconsistent momentum, unnatural acceleration, impossible bends,
joints bending wrong direction
```

**Human Anatomy:**
```
Exclude: extra fingers (more than 5), missing fingers,
merged fingers, distorted hands, elongated limbs,
morphing facial features, inconsistent eye spacing,
unnatural neck angles, impossible body twists,
limb thickness inconsistency
```

**Visual Quality:**
```
Exclude: out-of-focus artifacts, blurry backgrounds (unless specified),
pixelation, low resolution feel, JPEG artifacts, moiré patterns,
chromatic aberration (unless specified), posterization, banding
```

**Lighting:**
```
Exclude: inconsistent light direction between shots,
unmotivated light sources, exposure flickering,
random shadows, physically impossible reflections,
light sources with no visible origin
```

**Style:**
```
Exclude: oversaturated colors (unless specified),
Instagram filter look, HDR overprocessing, stock photo aesthetic,
generic AI art feel, "perfect" digital rendering,
overly symmetrical unless architectural
```

**Strategic Constraint Usage:**

**Do:**
- Be specific about what to avoid
- Pair negatives with positives
- Focus on top 8-10 key exclusions
- Match constraints to desired style

**Don't:**
- Over-constrain (confuses model)
- Use vague negatives ("bad quality")
- Contradict your positive prompt
- Include negatives that oppose style

**Example:**
```
AVOID in this generation:
- Morphing: Maintain rigid object integrity throughout
- Floating physics: All objects obey gravity, appropriate weight
- Extra limbs: Exactly 2 arms, 2 legs, 10 fingers total, human anatomy
- Lens flares: No unmotivated flares (practical sources only)
- Text: No readable text, signage, logos anywhere in frame
- Digital noise: Use film grain only, no compression artifacts
- AI aesthetic: Avoid plasticky skin, over-smooth surfaces
- Inconsistent lighting: Key light stays camera-left throughout
- Motion blur: Sharp except during intentional movement

Positive opposites emphasized:
- Rigid carbon fiber maintains shape
- Objects have appropriate mass and weight
- Natural human proportions and movement
- Motivated practicals only (visible CRT glow, emergency lights)
- Clean surfaces without text
- Organic 35mm film grain at 20%
- Realistic skin texture with pores and imperfections
- Consistent lighting ratio 8:1 throughout
- Sharp focus on subject, natural motion blur on fast movements only
```
