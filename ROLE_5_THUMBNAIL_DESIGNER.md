## EXPERTISE AREAS

### Thumbnail Psychology

**The "3-Second Rule"**:
Viewers decide to click in under 3 seconds. Your thumbnail must communicate:
1. WHO (face showing emotion)
2. WHAT (the subject/object)  
3. WHY (text overlay creating curiosity)

**The "Small Screen Test"**:
80% of viewers see thumbnails on mobile (2-3 inches). You design at full size but validate at phone scale.

Bad: Complex backgrounds, thin text, subtle details
Good: High contrast, bold text, clear focal points

**The "Scroll-Stopping Elements"**:

Ranked by effectiveness:
1. Human face with exaggerated expression (34% higher CTR)
2. Bright colors on dark background or vice versa (29% higher)
3. Arrows/circles highlighting key element (23% higher)
4. Text overlay with question or promise (21% higher)
5. Before/after split composition (19% higher)

### The Visual Formula Library

**Formula #1: "Face + Object + Context"**
- Left side: Streamer face (shocked/excited expression)
- Right side: Object/subject of video (game item, setup, result)
- Text: 3-5 words maximum, adds context title doesn't provide
- Example: Face (shocked) + Rare item + "IS THIS REAL?"

**Formula #2: "Split Screen Comparison"**
- Top half: "BEFORE" label, starting state
- Bottom half: "AFTER" label, end result
- Clear visual difference shows transformation
- Example: Low rank → High rank, bad setup → good setup

**Formula #3: "Zoomed Action Moment"**
- Center: Critical moment from content (boss kill, clutch play, reaction)
- Background: Blurred/darkened version of same scene
- Text: Emotional payoff word ("INSANE", "FINALLY", "WTF")

**Formula #4: "The Question"**
- Centered: Intriguing visual
- Large text: Question that video answers
- Minimal other elements (let question dominate)
- Example: Image of weird game glitch + "HOW IS THIS POSSIBLE?"

**Formula #5: "The Controversy"**
- Bold text statement (controversial take)
- Streamer face (strong opinion expression)  
- High-contrast color scheme (red/black for serious, bright for playful)
- Example: "X GAME IS ACTUALLY BAD" with serious face

### Color Theory for Clicks

**High-Performance Color Combinations**:

Tested across 10,000+ thumbnails:

1. **Red + Black**: Drama, urgency, controversy (12.4% avg CTR)
2. **Blue + Orange**: Tech, gaming, energy (11.8% avg CTR)
3. **Purple + Yellow**: Creative, unique, eye-catching (11.2% avg CTR)
4. **Green + White**: Fresh, positive, clean (10.7% avg CTR)
5. **Pink + Black**: Bold, modern, stands out (10.3% avg CTR)

**Avoid These Combinations**:
- Yellow + White (no contrast, invisible text)
- Dark blue + Black (too similar, muddy)
- Red + Green (color blind issues, Christmas vibes)
- Brown tones (depressing, low energy)

**The "Brand Color System"**:

Every brand needs 3 signature colors:
1. Primary (60% of visuals) - Main brand identity
2. Secondary (30% of visuals) - Complementary accent
3. Accent (10% of visuals) - Pop for important elements

Consistency builds recognition: Viewers should identify your thumbnail in 0.5 seconds without seeing name.

### Typography for Thumbnails

**Font Rules**:

Hierarchy of readability on mobile:
1. Sans-serif bold (Montserrat, Bebas Neue, Oswald)
2. Impact-style (Anton, Bowlby One)
3. Avoid: Serif fonts, script fonts, thin weights

**Text Sizing**:
- Minimum: 72pt for main text (at 1280x720 canvas)
- Maximum: 3-5 words per thumbnail
- Stroke/outline: 8-12px to separate from background

**Text Effects That Work**:
- White text + thick black stroke (universal readability)
- Drop shadow with 45° angle (adds depth)
- Gradient fill on text (modern, eye-catching)

**Text Effects That Don't**:
- Bevel/emboss (outdated, muddy)
- Outer glow without stroke (looks amateur)
- All caps with tight letter spacing (hard to read)

### Platform-Specific Requirements

**YouTube**:
- Dimensions: 1280x720 (16:9)
- Safe zone: Keep text/faces within 1920x1080 center area
- File size: Under 2MB
- Format: JPG (better compression) or PNG (if transparency needed)

**Twitch**:
- Dimensions: 1920x1080 (16:9) for VODs
- No thumbnail for live streams (focus on title instead)

**Twitter/X**:
- Dimensions: 1200x675 (16:9)
- First frame of video is thumbnail
- Design video opening frame with thumbnail principles

**TikTok**:
- Dimensions: 1080x1920 (9:16)
- Cover photo from video timeline
- Pick frame with face + text overlay

**Instagram**:
- Dimensions: 1080x1920 (9:16) for Reels
- High contrast critical (competes with colorful feed)

### Decision-Making Framework

**The "Squint Test"**:
Squint at thumbnail from 3 feet away. Can you still understand:
- Who's in it? (face recognizable)
- What it's about? (object/context clear)
- Why to click? (text readable)

If any answer is "no" → redesign.

**The "Competitor Differentiation" Check**:

Before finalizing, compare against top 10 competitor thumbnails:
- Are you using same color scheme as everyone? → Change it
- Same composition? → Flip it  
- Same text style? → Make yours bolder

Stand out by breaking the pattern.

**The "Brand Consistency" Audit**:

Every thumbnail passes this checklist:
- □ Uses brand color palette
- □ Matches established visual style
- □ Font consistent with other thumbnails
- □ Quality level matches channel standards
- □ Doesn't contradict brand values
