## II. Advanced Techniques for Narrative and Consistency

### A. Multi-Shot Prompting and Temporal Segmentation

The most advanced technique for long-form narrative is treating the prompt as a **script with Timestamp Segmentation** [8].

*   **Principle:** Break the video into 4-second "chunks" for maximum reliability, then stitch them in post-production [4]. The prompt for the entire sequence is written as a single script, with clear shot breaks.
*   **Structure:**
    ```
    [SHOT 1: Full 8-Component Prompt for Scene A]. [SHOT 2: Full 8-Component Prompt for Scene B]. [TRANSITION: Match Cut on Motion].
    ```
*   **Character Consistency:** To maintain the same character, the **ID/Description** must be repeated in every shot block. Experts suggest using a unique, highly detailed description that includes clothing, specific physical traits (e.g., scar, unique jewelry), and a name [7].

### B. Dialogue and Audio Integration

Sora 2's enhanced audio capabilities require dedicated prompting for high-fidelity results [9].

*   **Dialogue Handling:** Place short lines of dialogue in a dedicated block to ensure the most accurate lip-sync and verbatim delivery.
*   **Soundscape:** Specify the atmosphere and sound cues to enhance realism.
    *   **Example:** `Sound of distant sirens, water dripping from a leaky pipe, muffled jazz music from an upstairs apartment.`

### C. The "Less is More" Principle for Simple Shots

While the 8-Component Script is for high-control, the research validates that **shorter, high-signal prompts** are better for quick, creative B-roll [1].

*   **Focus:** Let the AI be the cinematographer. Focus only on the **Subject, Action, and Environment.**
*   **Example:** `A jazz performance at the American Bar. Let the AI direct.`
