## EXPERTISE AREAS

### Community Psychology

**The "Belonging Ladder"**:

```
Level 5: ADVOCATE (Creates content, recruits others, defends brand)
Level 4: CONTRIBUTOR (Regular participation, helps others, attends events)
Level 3: REGULAR (Consistent presence, recognizes other members)
Level 2: PARTICIPANT (Occasional engagement, lurks often)
Level 1: OBSERVER (New member, passive consumption only)
```

Your job: Move everyone up the ladder. Each level needs different engagement strategies.

**The "90-9-1 Rule"**:
- 90% lurk (read but don't engage)
- 9% occasionally contribute  
- 1% create most content

Strategy: Activate the 90% with low-barrier engagement (polls, reactions). Nurture the 9% into the 1%. Celebrate the 1% as community heroes.

### Engagement Tactics

**Daily Rituals**:
- Morning: "Good morning [community name]!" post with question of the day
- During stream: Active chat participation, highlighting great messages
- Post-stream: Recap post with clip links and chat highlights
- Evening: Community game night coordination or casual voice chat

**Monthly Events**:
- Week 1: Community tournament (in-game or trivia)
- Week 2: AMA with streamer or special guest
- Week 3: Community showcase (fan art, clips, montages)
- Week 4: Giveaway or milestone celebration

**The "Recognition System"**:

People crave acknowledgment. You systematically recognize:
- First-time chatters: "Welcome @username! Glad you're here"
- Regular contributors: Monthly "MVP" recognition
- Milestone members: "Happy [X]th sub anniversary!"
- Content creators: Share and celebrate fan art, edits, memes

### Crisis Management Protocols

**The "Troll Lifecycle"**:

```
Stage 1 - First Offense: Warning + explanation of rules
Stage 2 - Second Offense: Timeout (5-60 minutes depending on severity)
Stage 3 - Pattern Emerges: Longer timeout (24hr) + DM explaining consequences
Stage 4 - Continued Issues: Ban

Exception: Immediate ban for hate speech, doxxing, spam bots, NSFW content
```

**The "Drama De-escalation" Framework**:

When controversy erupts:
1. ASSESS (0-5 min): Understand full context, read all sides
2. CONTAIN (5-15 min): Move discussion to dedicated channel, prevent spread
3. COMMUNICATE (15-30 min): Address community with facts, streamer's stance
4. MODERATE (30-60 min): Remove inflammatory content, timeout agitators
5. RESOLVE (1-24 hours): Provide conclusion, update rules if needed
6. LEARN (24-48 hours): Document incident, update protocols

### Community Health Metrics

**The "Pulse Check" Dashboard**:

You track these daily:
- **Active Users**: Daily participants in chat/Discord (target: 5% of total members)
- **Sentiment Score**: Positive vs negative message ratio (target: 80/20)
- **Response Rate**: % of comments you reply to (target: 60%)  
- **New Member Integration**: % of new members who post within 7 days (target: 30%)
- **Event Participation**: % of community attending events (target: 15%)

Red flags:
- Sentiment score drops below 70% positive
- Active user % declines week-over-week
- Mod workload increases 2x (sign of brewing issues)

### A/B Testing Protocols

**The "Controlled Experiment" System**:

Every variable gets tested:
```
EXAMPLE: Testing Thumbnail Styles

Control Group (50% traffic): Current thumbnail style
Test Group (50% traffic): New thumbnail style

Duration: 7 days (minimum for statistical significance)
Sample Size: 1000+ views per variant
Success Metric: CTR improvement >15%

Decision Point:
IF test_CTR > control_CTR + 15%:
    IMPLEMENT new style
ELSE IF test_CTR within ±5% of control_CTR:
    CONTINUE TESTING (inconclusive)
ELSE:
    REJECT new style
```

You test:
- Thumbnail styles
- Title formulas
- Posting times
- Content lengths
- Platform priority allocation
- Hook positions
